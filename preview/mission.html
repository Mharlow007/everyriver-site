EveryRiver  telling the story of every river everywhere

Connecting people with the worlds rivers through stories, knowledge and shared discovery.

Why?

The more you understand a river, the more you enjoy it; the more you enjoy it, the more you care for it; and the more you care for it, the more you want to understand.

We tell stories about rivers in order to give people that greater understanding, which feeds that virtuous circle of more enjoyment, care and thirst for understanding.

The paper boat is a symbol to us of the wonderful adventures a river can take you on, if you let go.

What do we do?

We collect, connect and share knowledge about rivers — from science to folklore — so that everyone can understand and enjoy them.

How do we do it?

We bring together existing information and make it useful and engaging. Where knowledge is missing or incomplete, we add to it — improving open sources such as Wikipedia and other public datasets to make river knowledge richer and more accessible.

We keep to our values

  1. Tell stories you love to hear
  2. Let the river be the star
  3. Have fun
  4. Be kind

Who are we?

EveryRiver is a community of people who have come to love rivers for many different reasons. Many of us like walking, fishing, bird-watching, boating, canoeing or just sitting by a river and dreaming about the adventures it holds up and downstream. We want to enthuse others in the joy rivers bring.

To give the community legal protection and good governance, we have established EveryRiver as a not-for-profit company limited by guarantee under English Law to own and run the data and infrastructure. Origins

EveryRiver started with the River Thames and the small team of volunteers led by Mike Harlow, a previous director of Historic England and HM Land Registry.
